One of the most promising things quantum computer offers is its various applications in chemistry. With fully coherent quantum computers, we will be able to simulate molecules, and understand what goes on at the quantum scale, and possible accelerate the drug discovery process. However, we are still in the NISQ (Noisy Intermediate Scale Quantum) era, and our quantum computers don't have long enough coherent runtimes to solve these problems. This is where the Variational Quantum Eigensolver (or VQE, for short) comes in. The VQE algorithm is a quantum machine learning algorithm that has a much shallower circuit than QPEs and has many applications in chemistry in the near term. I used this algorithm to find the ground state energy (and the associated bond length) of Lithium Hydride. This is done by finding the expectation value of the molecular Hamiltonian, which by the variational principle, must be equal to or greater than the eigenvalue associated with the hamiltonian's matrix, H.
